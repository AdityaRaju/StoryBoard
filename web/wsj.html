Tata Motors Earnings: Jaguar Land Rover Drives Gain

NEW DELHI— Tata Motors Ltd. 500570.BY +0.90% 's profit rose for the first time in four quarters, with a strong performance at unit Jaguar Land Rover Automotive PLC helping the group mask weak demand at home in India.

Consolidated net profit at the Mumbai-based maker of the Nano minicar and Prima heavy-duty truck surged 71% to 35.42 billion rupees ($559.3 million) for the fiscal second quarter ended Sept. 30, from 20.74 billion rupees a year earlier. The latest results exceeded the forecast of 27.66 billion rupees in a FactSet survey of analysts.

Sales increased 30% to 557.01 billion rupees.

Jaguar Land Rover has become a crucial profit source for Tata Motors since it purchased the U.K.-based luxury marquees from Ford Motor Co. F +1.81% in 2008. The unit has introduced several new models, such as the Range Rover and Range Rover Evoque SUVs and the Jaguar XF and F-Type cars, in the past few years. The new vehicles have helped drive Jaguar Land Rover's sales, especially in China—its largest market, with a nearly 24% share of sales.

Sales of Tata-brand cars, sport-utility vehicles, trucks and buses at the Indian parent have continued to fall because of an overall slowdown in the domestic market, intense competition from new and existing players, and a lack of new products, especially in emerging segments such as compact SUVs.

To reverse declining sales in India, Tata Motors has introduced upgraded versions of the Nano as well as the Indigo compact car and the Safari Storme and Sumo SUVs.

Jamshed Dadabhoy and Arvind Sharma, analysts at Citigroup Global Markets, said in a recent report that they remain "skeptical about Tata Motors' overall passenger-vehicle portfolio given its jaded brand image and lack of new models."

Max Warburton, analyst at Bernstein Research, said Tata Motors' stand-alone business in India is "under severe pressure," because the company is saddled with uncompetitive products and faces increased foreign competition.

Its India operations incurred a net loss of 8.04 billion rupees for the most recent quarter, compared with a net profit of 8.67 billion rupees a year earlier. Sales in India fell 29% to 88.68 billion rupees.

The performance of Jaguar Land Rover, however, continued to improve. The unit said its net profit rose 66% to £507 million ($812 million). Sales at the unit increased 40% to £4.61 billion.

The number of vehicles despatched from Jaguar Land Rover's factories rose 32% to 101,931. Meanwhile, sales of Tata-brand of vehicles slumped 33% to 150,930 autos.

"Slowdown in economic activity, low level of transport trade and infrastructure activity, a tight financing environment and diesel-price increases have led to very low vehicle demand in India," C. Ramakrishnan, Tata Motors' chief financial officer, said at a news conference in Mumbai. "The competitive intensity has impacted the overall margin."

Jaguar Land Rover improved its earnings margin before interest, tax, depreciation and amortization to 17.8% in the July-September quarter from 14.8% a year earlier.

At Tata Motors' Indian operations, the Ebitda margin narrowed to 2% from 5.9%.
